Lily-Rose Depp and the Weeknd Respond to Chloe Finemans The Idol Parody

Publish date: 2024-05-06

As the new show taking over Succession’s coveted 9 p.m. ET time slot on HBO, The Idol has been pretty widely discussed since its premiere last Sunday, June 4. The new Sam Levinson drama follows a “self-help guru” and underground cult leader named Tedros, played by the Weeknd (a.k.a. Abel Tesfaye), who influences rising pop star Jocelyn, played by Lily-Rose Depp.

The series has earned mixed reviews thus far, but what we do know for certain is that Saturday Night Live’s Chloe Fineman might just be our generation’s best impressionist, which she proved once again when she dropped a *chef's kiss* parody of Depp’s Jocelyn on Instagram last week. Observe:

“My audition for THE IDOL (must have got lost in the mail) @theidol 🚬🚬 (@lilyrose_depp is a GODDESS of talent),” Fineman captioned the clip, which featured many iconic moments inspired by direct lines and moments from the show’s first episode, plus some that simply riff on its general vibe. “Anyway, I met this guy last night, his name is Tedros. He was dressed in a Batcave, but I think he’s gonna, like, change my career,” Fineman-as-Jocelyn dished.

In other scenes, Fineman puffed on four cigarettes simultaneously and wore everything from a backward bodysuit to a red robe that she put on her face in a nod to the episode’s ending, in which Tedros helps Jocelyn achieve a creative breakthrough by way of *checks notes* erotic asphyxiation.

Depp and Tesfaye acknowledged Fineman’s video publicly. “I’m loling 🤣🤣🤣🤣 ….. and ur makeup looks bomb,” Depp wrote in the comments. Tesfaye, for his part, had this to say:

Though the series stars have certainly contributed to the hype around the show, larger questions about the working environment on The Idol have made headlines recently, driven in part by a Rolling Stone exposé from March that claimed things generally went “off the rails” behind the scenes of the show. What do we know for sure? Though the show was originally announced in June 2021 and green-lit for a six-episode pilot season for November of that year, in April 2022 the release timeline was pushed due to “major changes.” A number of crew members walked away from the project, including then-director Amy Seimetz. (Following her departure, Sam Levinson became the lead director on the project.)

Of the changes, HBO said, “The Idol’s creative team continues to build, refine, and evolve their vision for the show and they have aligned on a new creative direction. The production will be adjusting its cast and crew accordingly to best serve this new approach to the series. We look forward to sharing more information soon.”
