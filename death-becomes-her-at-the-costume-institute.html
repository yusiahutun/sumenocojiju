Death Becomes Her at the Costume Institute

Publish date: 2024-04-17

Picture: Goldie Hawn in Death Becomes Her. Image from Rex Features 

The Charles James: Beyond Fashion exhibition may still be in full swing, but there’s already a new show to get excited about at the Costume Institute, albeit with a slightly more subdued theme.
 
Opening on 21 October, Death Becomes Her: A Century of Mourning Attire, will showcase the clothes worn by grieving families in the years 1815–1915 and will include pieces from the most famous widow of the period, Queen Victoria. Alongside some 30 items, there will also be photographs and daguerreotypes depicting bereavement ceremonies.
 
The exhibition marks the first autumn show for the museum in seven years, so the dark theme seems particularly apt. We’re expecting black, black and more black.
