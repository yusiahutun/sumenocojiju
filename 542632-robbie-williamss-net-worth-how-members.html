What is Robbie Williams’ net worth?

Celebrity Net Worth and The Richest estimate Williams' net worth to be between $200 and $300 million. His journey to making millions started in 1996 when he departed from Take That to begin a solo career. So, how did he make his money?

Williams has sold over 75 million records worldwide. In 2002, he signed a $150 million (£80 million) contract with EMI, the largest contract in British music history. In 2006, he set a world record by selling 1.6 million concert tickets daily.

Solo music career

Robbie has established himself as a prominent figure in the music industry with a multifaceted career spanning over three decades. He rose to fame as a popular boy band Take That member from 1990 to 1995 before embarking on a successful solo career in 1996.

His debut studio album, Life thru a Lens, began his solo journey and featured the iconic song Angels. Subsequently, his second album, I've Been Expecting You, solidified his solo success with hit singles like Millennium and She's the One.

The singer has achieved remarkable milestones throughout his career, including seven UK No. 1 singles and Guinness World Records for ticket sales during his Close Encounters Tour. His discography boasts seven UK number-one singles and twelve studio albums, most of which also topped the charts.

Business ventures

The personality has also delved into the business world. In 2009, he launched Farrell Music, a company that became the hub for all his music publishing rights. This move allowed complete ownership and control over his songs while partnering with Notting Hill Music for administration services.

According to Stroke on Trent Live, in February 2006, Robbie Williams bought £240,000 worth of shares in the club Port Vale, making him the largest shareholder. Unfortunately, the club entered administration on 9 March 2012.

As a result, Williams lost all of his shares in the club and received only a fraction of his original investment back. One of Williams' most successful business ventures was founding his record label, Farrell Music Limited, in 2007.

Robbie Williams’ houses

According to See and So, the singer's property portfolio includes various luxurious estates in different locations. In London, he owns the Woodland Mansion in Holland Park, which he purchased in 2013 for £17.5 million.

This 47-room mansion is situated among other celebrity homes. Williams also had a conflict with his neighbour, Jimmy Page, over building an underground swimming pool on this property. In 2015, he acquired a sprawling 10-bedroom property in Beverly Hills for £29 million.

The British singer later sold the house to Drake for at least £31 million. In 2021, Williams invested in a dream villa in Zurich, Switzerland. Robbie Williams' house in Switzerland is estimated at £24 million and offers a scenic retreat with a traditional-style villa and a swimming pool.

Car collection

Besides houses, Robbie Williams previously had an impressive car collection. The singer indulged in a lavish spending spree, purchasing five cars despite not having a driver's license.

He bought a Porsche, a Ferrari, a "Mercedes Russian gangster car," an Aston Martin, and one additional car he could not recall. Each car cost around £170,000, totalling £1 million for all five vehicles.

His admiration for Elton John's impressive car collection inspired the extravagant purchase. Despite his significant investment in these luxury cars, Robbie never developed an interest in learning how to drive. Ultimately, he sold off the cars shortly after their delivery.
