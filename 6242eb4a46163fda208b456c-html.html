IRS Tax Refunds: Where can you cash your Tax Refund Checks?

Publish date: 2024-05-13

It is a brilliant feeling to get a large tax refund check in the mail from the United States Treasury. But, not everyone knows how to actually cash it.

Here, in this guide, we run you through the places where you can cash your tax refund checks.

Cashing tax refund checks in banks

First up we have banks. If you already have a bank account, your bank will cash your tax refund checks without charging a fee.

If you don't have a bank account, you can still cash the check at any bank, and some banks will charge a small fee for the service as you are not a customer.

This exact amount depends on the bank itself, but these fees are usually less than $10.

Cashing tax refund checks in check-cashing businesses

Second up are check-cashing businesses. These businesses charge a fee for their service, and this fee depends on the value of the check.

The fee could be a percentage of the check, or it could also be a flat fee for certain amounts which gradually increases for larger amounts.

Many small grocery stores also offer this service, as well as convenience stores.

Cashing tax refund checks in large retailers

A number of large retailers also offer check-cashing services, for example Wal-Mart and Publix.

These stores often charge significantly smaller fees than you would have to pay at check-cashing businesses.

Some waive the fees altogether if you make a purchase at the store and then use the check for payment.

Cashing tax refund checks with prepaid cards

With regards to prepaid cards, you can receive these instead of money when you cash your tax refund check.

However, these cards are not linked to an account (unlike bank debit cards), so there is no protection in the event that it is stolen, and you can only spend the amount of money that you have loaded.

These cards are available at a number of locations, for example retail stores, as well as convenience stores.
