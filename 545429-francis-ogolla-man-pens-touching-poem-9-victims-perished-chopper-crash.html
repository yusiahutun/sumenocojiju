Francis Ogolla: Man Pens Touching Poem to 9 Victims who Perished in Chopper Crash

Publish date: 2024-07-14
  • A Kenyan script writer, Kipkurui Wilberforce, penned a tearjerking poem mourning the eight victims who perished in the chopper crash alongside CDF Francis Ogolla
  • In his poem, Kipkirui talked about how life is short, noting that one day, we would all die, and our memories would fade
  • Kenyans flooded his post with touching messages, with many condoling with the families of the eight victims

Tributes continue to pour in for the late Chief of Defence Forces Francis Ogolla and eight others who died in a chopper crash on April 18.

William Ruto announces 3 days of mourning

The head of state confirmed that the ill-fated chopper had 11 occupants on board at the time it crashed in Kaben, the border of Elgeyo Marakwet and West Pokot counties. Nine people died, while two survived.

The officials who died alongside Ogolla are Brigadier Swale Saidi, Colonel Duncan Keittany, Lieutenant Colonel David Sawe, Major George Benson Magondu, Captain Sora Mohamed, Captain Hillary Litali, Senior Sergeant John Kinyua Mureithi, Sergeant Cliphonce Omondi, and Sergeant Rose Nyawira.

The ill-fated Kenya Air Force Huey Helicopter was ferrying its occupants to the bandit-stricken north rift region on a working visit.

They were visiting troops deployed in the region under Operation Maliza Uhalifu, besides their inspection of the renovation works in a host of schools there.

During the address, Ruto declared three days of national mourning.

Kenyan writer's tribute to Francis Ogolla, KDF soldiers

Script writer and teacher by profession, Kipkurui Wilberforce penned a heartbreaking poem mourning the nine victims, moving Kenyans to tears.

In his poem, Kipkirui talked about how life is short, noting that one day, we would all die, and our memories would fade.

"Sad. Our life is shortLike a firefly blinking once in the night. We're not really here very longWe don’t really matter that much.And nothing that we do lasts. So, eventually, we will fade. Our works will fade. Our children will fade. Our thoughts will fade. This planet will fade. The sun will fade. It will all be gone," he posted, accompanied by the last photo of CDF Ogolla and some KDF officials before the crash.
