Why Kendall Jenner and Bad Bunny Broke Up After Less Than a Year of Dating

Publish date: 2024-04-14

After less than a year of dating, Kendall Jenner and Bad Bunny have reportedly called it quits.

People reported on December 17 that the model and the musician have ended their relationship. The two first sparked romance rumors in February, after they were seen on a double date with Hailey and Justin Bieber in Los Angeles. They were last publicly seen together at a Saturday Night Live after-party in October.

An insider for Entertainment Tonight gave some insight into what led to the couple’s split, revealing that the two stars knew from the start that their relationship had an expiration date.

“Kendall and Bad Bunny have been doing their own things lately, and things slowly started to fizzle out between them,” the insider said. “They both knew going into this that it likely wouldn’t be a forever type of relationship, and that was mutually understood from the get-go.”

It also comes down to timing. The source explained, “They have crazy-busy schedules and know they’re still young and have a lot more to experience individually before settling down.”

Getty Images

Despite no longer being romantically involved, the source said that there “isn’t any negativity between them and they still want the best for one another.” In particular, the model’s family “still thinks highly of [Bad Bunny].” The source added, “They just want her to be with whoever makes her happy, treats her with love and respect, and someone that understands and can handle the attention that comes along with dating her with poise and grace.”

Jenner previously talked about her approach to relationships in an interview for Harper’s Bazaar’s September 2023 cover story.

“I love really hard, and I love without apology,” she said at the time. “I don’t like goodbyes, and I will fight to not have to say goodbye. I will always fight for relationships. I’ve been that way since I was little, although I was shy and sometimes very closed off. I don’t give up on anything. Some people aren’t willing to meet me at that level. But that’s okay. I’d rather do that than shut myself off to something and not give it a proper chance.”
