Sydney home shaped like a penis dubbed 'Buckingham Phallus' gets new owners

Publish date: 2024-03-20

It may have been a hard sell, but real estate agents have beaten off stiff competition in the property market to find new owners for this suggestively shaped home.

The Church Point property in Northern Sydney drew international attention last year because the floor plan bears a striking resemblance to a penis - earning it the nickname 'Buckingham Phallus.'

With four bedrooms, a two-car garage and two bathrooms, the new owners saw the home as the whole package - despite being on the market for an entire year.

A couple have snapped up a property in Northern Sydney which drew national attention because its floor plan bears a striking resemblance to a penis

While the residence has been dubbed 'the penis house' by neighbours, the real estate agent said the home was apparently snapped up by an inner west couple for an undisclosed price

The double brick property was designed by architect Stan Symonds Sherwin and built back in 1958

But Phil Vanstone, an agent from LJ Hooker Mona Vale, believes Sherwin's suggestive floorplan was intentional

The home was apparently snapped up by an inner west couple for an undisclosed price, reports Daily Telegraph.

'They plan to restore it to its original state, and I have even put them in contact with the original architect,' Agent Stephen Allen, of Belle Property Mona Vale.

He said the home grabbed headlines internationally when it went up for sale last year.
