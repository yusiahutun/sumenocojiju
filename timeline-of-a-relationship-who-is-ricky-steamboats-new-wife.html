Timeline of a Relationship: Who Is Ricky Steamboats New Wife?

Publish date: 2024-04-07

Ricky Steamboat is a former pro wrestler. In 2009, his long-time rival, Ric Flair, inducted him into the WWE Hall of Fame. He was a WWF Intercontinental Champion once, NWA World Heavyweight Champion once, a four-time United States Heavyweight Champion, a four-time Television Champion, and an eight-time WCW World Tag Team Champion.

After an eight-year retirement, the wrestling legend returned to WWE in 2005 as a road agent. In 2006, he also officiated the WWE Championship match between John Cena and Edge. Ricky took on the role of referee once more in July 2007, this time for a title match between John Cena and Randy Orton.

Ricky Steamboat’s Biography

Richard Henry Blood is the real name of the former WWF Intercontinental Champion. He was born in West Point, New York on February 28, 1953.

He trained under Verne Gagne and made his name in the Carolinas, the old Mid-Atlantic territory. Because of his resemblance to wrestling legend Sammy Steamboat, he was given the name “Ricky Steamboat.”

Ricky Steamboat- Relationship, Married Life

Sandi McAlister Blood is Ricky “The Dragon” Steamboat’s wife. In 2017, the couple married. He had previously been married four times. On January 8, 1977, the first to Maureen Muriel Powers. On June 16, 1980, the relationship ended.

He was married to Debra Blood (14 June 1981-15 April 1985), Bonnie Blood (18 May 1985-15 January 2003), and Claudia Sobieski after his first divorce (21 August 2004-2012).

Son

With his third wife, Bonne Blood, the retired wrestler has a son named Richard Blood Jr. On July 7, 1987, he was born. Richard Blood Jr. is a wrestler like his father. On July 25, 2008, he made his debut under the ring name Richie Steamboat.

Richie was the second FCW Grand Slam Champion, having previously won the Florida Heavyweight Championship, the Florida Tag Team Championship, and the Jack Brisco 15 Championship.

After seven years of professional wrestling, he retired on April 15, 2015.

Fascinating Facts

  • In 1977, he was named “Inside Wrestling” Rookie of the Year.
  • Eddie Graham, a wrestling promoter, gave Ricky his ring name.
  • He is of Japanese ancestry.
  • Ricky fought Randy Savage for the WWE Intercontinental Championship, which he won at WrestleMania III (1987)
  • He is approximately 6 feet tall (1.83 meters).
