Joe Machi Bio, Age, Height, Illness, Net Worth, Wiki

Publish date: 2024-01-28

Joe Machi is an American actor, writer, and stand-up comedian known for You Didn’t Want to Know, Comics, and Crashing. Joe has earned a considerable fan following with fame through funnier jokes filled with humor.

He mainly performs in various comedy clubs and various tours throughout the country. Despite being too young, his humor on jokes is on par with so many famous comedians and he quite often makes appearances on various talk shows which gives a huge boost to his career.

Joe achieved yet another milestone in his journey as he quite often gets featured on the popular show Gutfeld! on the Fox Network with Greg Gutfeld which also adds quite a hefty amount of fan following to his social media. 

Joe Machi Bio, Age, Height, Parents, Wife, Net Worth, Wikipedia

Joe Machi was born on 23rd June 1979 in State College, Pennsylvania. He mainly grew up in the same neighborhood but later moved to New York to pursue his comedy career. His parents are Frank Machi who is a store manager, and his mother Catherine Machi who worked as a teacher at a Catholic school.

His parents have mentioned that Joe has always been making fun and jokes, entertaining everyone around him since a young age. Joe attended and completed his high school at State College Area School and then later went to study at Penn State University in Pennsylvania where he earned his Bachelor’s degree.

Career

After finishing his graduation, Joe didn’t suddenly pursue comedy and make a successful career out of it. Instead, he initially worked as a human resource assistant at a private company and customer service manager at a local supermarket. He even worked part-time to make ends meet.

Then later in 2006, Machi moved to New York so that he could pursue comedy on a much larger scale. Subsequently, he was seen performing stand-up comedies in various shows such as Late Night with Jimmy Fallon, The CBC’s Just For Laughs Gala and has been a panelist on The Fox News program, Red Eye.

In 2009 he was also seen in a short drama series titled Comics. He was also one of 10 comics who were selected to perform at the 2012 Montreal Just For Laughs New Faces showcase in Canada. The same year he also was a finalist for the 2012 Andy Kaufman Award.

Soon enough he became quite famous in the industry and was also mentioned in prominent news channels such as The New York Times, NY Daily News, NY Post, and Time Out New York. In 2010, Machi won the NY Underground Comedy Festival’s Emerging Comics Contest.

Apart from being a comedian, in 2017 he appeared in the comedy-drama series Crashing and in the 2018 series Didn’t Want to Know. Machi has become a staple on New York Comedy Club and alternate comedy shows.

Joe Machi Illness

So many viewers think that Joe is suffering from some kind of illness because of his high-pitched voice. Although, he is completely fit and fine and doesn’t suffer from any sort of health issues. He clarifies this on Jimmy Fallon’s Tonight’s Show. He said,

“I’m not intentionally nervous; I just don’t fight to be nervous. It works with what I do, anyway. By now you’re realizing I have a higher-pitched voice than most men.”
