The gut health protocol Margot Robbie followed filming Barbie

Publish date: 2024-07-28

It’s well versed that Hollywood stars work with personal trainers and nutritionists while filming movies. What’s lesser known is that skin health experts can be called upon to help actors achieve their best skin for roles – naturally a priority for anyone playing a living Barbie doll.

Enter Jasmina Vico, the London-based skin pro who counts Jodie Comer among her loyal clients and – now – the Barbie cast. Working closely with the film’s make-up artist Ivana Primorac, Vico helped achieved the skin results for the movie’s desired look which was, essentially, perfection. From Margot Robbie to America Ferrera, Emma Mackey and Hari Nef, she treated the Barbies to her bespoke skin and wellbeing treatments throughout filming around one-to-two times a week. For Robbie, this meant a combination of her signature Vico Glow Laser, LED therapy, lymphatic drainage, cold laser, pressure point massage and her expert ‘gut x skin protocol' – so Vico tells us. Clearly, “Margot is very disciplined”.

Between appointments, Robbie was taking probiotics and other anti-inflammatory supplements, Vico explains from her clinic in Fitzrovia. These included the expert’s own Vico Skin 3-6-9 Supplements trilogy: Phyto Energy, Skin Frequency and Gut Vibration. “Margot was also eating fermented foods like sauerkraut, kimchi, and kefir, all kinds of bitter foods, and berries.” It’s all to do with “promoting the healing and reducing the stress in the body,” she explains, which is also why she had Robbie drinking organic milk thistle tea. “It helps with flushing the liver which makes the skin better because it produces more glutathione which helps with luminosity and brightness,” she explains. “They [the Barbies] were all having that tea.”

Han Myung-Gu//Getty Images

It's this inside-out approach to skincare – and the lifestyle it promotes – that stands Vico’s treatments apart. “It didn’t make any sense for me just to treat the skin! Skin is connected to your body; you’ve got to include the diet and lifestyle.” Not that the stars objected. “Most of them were never really aware of the gut-skin axis. Most of the time when they have a skin treatment it would just be for their face and that would be it.” But this was no ‘red carpet treatment’.

"They’re not living dolls – and nobody is. You’ve got to do the work!"

Indeed, another focus beyond bolstering the Barbies’ gut heath was counteracting their stress. Vico had them doing “meditation and breathing; The Wim Hof Method” (which combines breath work and cold therapy), and during treatments she placed a device called Sensate on the actors' chests, which uses sonic resonance to calm the body's nervous system.

Robbie has remained a client of Vico’s “coming even after the filming when she’s here in London”. “It’s kind of a lifestyle,” the expert feels, noting that there’s no quick fix when it comes to achieving complexion perfection. “We always want a magic wand, but good skin is a journey. They’re not living dolls – and nobody is. You’ve got to do the work! You’ve got to be disciplined and do the work to bring you closer to that level.”
