What is Retinol?

As per our findings, retinoids is an umbrella term to refer to a bunch of vitamin A-derived molecules like retinol. According to a research paper published on NCBI, this key ingredient found in moisturizing creams tends to influence a variety of cellular processes like cell surface alterations, cellular growth, and differentiation, and is effective enough in the treatment of photoaging and aging. With consistent use, significant improvement is noticed in the appearance of fine wrinkles, lines, and dark spots.

How Does a Retinol Cream Work?

Another study published in the National Library of Medicine reported that retinol is a fat-soluble component extracted from vitamin A, which has the power to treat mature skin concerns. Retinoids with high concentration levels possess keratolytic activities that decrease cell renewal and improve acne marks and wrinkles. In addition, aims to brighten your complexion and boost the health and quality of your overall skin. A cream with retinol promptly executes skin-cell turnover that clears dull dead cells faster, strengthens the skin barrier, and supports collagen production. This process is largely responsible for giving your skin a firmer look and a bouncier feel.

    How to Choose the Best Retinol Creams?

    Here are a few aspects that you should consider before splurging on the best retinol cream:

    Skin Type: Different types of skin require different day or overnight treatments. Right from normal, dry, and combination skin to acne-prone skin, retinol is one such ingredient that works wonders. For those who possess dry skin, choose a retinol cream that is loaded with hydrating ingredients like glycerin and hyaluronic acid. Whereas, the ones battling with oily skin should pick a cream that is crafted with oil-free ingredients like salicylic acid. 

    Concentration Levels of Retinol: Pay heed to the concentration levels of retinol present in the cream if you are a first-time user. Based on our first-hand research, there are creams with varied concentration levels of retinol like 1%, 0.5%, .0.3%, and 0.25%. Beginners can choose creams with the lowest retinol concentration and gradually incorporate the ones with higher strengthens. 

    Ingredients: Retinol is known for rejuvenating, improving, nourishing, and hydrating your skin by reversing the signs of aging. It is advisable to vouch for creams that are enriched with pure retinol. For added skin benefits, you can pick creams infused with hyaluronic acid, glycerin, shea butter, salicylic acid, cocoa butter, vitamins C and E, cica, squalene, chamomile, calendula, licorice root, bakuchiol, fruit extracts, etc. Refrain from buying creams that contain parabens, alcohol, phthalates, synthetic fragrances, and artificial dyes. 

    Texture: Retinol cream is found in varied textures namely cream, gel, serum, or lotion. As per your skin type and comfort level, you may choose any of the above textures. Gels and serums tend to be very light on the skin whereas creams are more rich and hydrating.

    Directions for Usage: Always pay heed to the directions for the usage of the retinol cream. Some creams can be incorporated into your AM regime as long as you wear sunscreen. The majority of them are meant to be incorporated into PM skincare regimes for overnight skin renewal. If you ask us, a retinol cream works best when applied at night.

    Tips to Prevent Irritation

    • You may experience mild redness, a warm, tingling sensation, or skin exfoliation. As per our intensive research, these symptoms are normal and temporary signs that indicate the formula is working.
    • If irritation and burning sensation persist or cause skin discomfort, immediately limit the usage of the cream to once every alternate day until your skin adjusts. 
    • When applying the cream, skip your eyelids. If the cream comes in contact with your eyes, rinse with water. 
    • Stop using the cream and reach out to the doctor if rashes or excessive irritation develop and lasts for a prolonged period of time.

    FAQ's

    How often should I use retinol cream?

    You should use a retinol cream daily at night for optimum results once your skin adjusts to retinol.

    Are there any side effects of retinol cream?

    Yes, you can experience side effects like irritation, redness, and allergies.

    How long does it take to see the results after using a retinol cream?

    It takes 4 to 10 weeks to see the results after using a retinol cream.

    Should some people avoid cream with retinol?

    If you are prone to allergies or have extremely sensitive skin, then avoid creams with retinol.

    Are retinol creams good for all skin types?

    No, retinol creams are not recommended for those who have overly sensitive or hypersensitive skin.

    Should you moisturize your skin after applying retinol?

    Yes, you should moisturize your skin after applying retinol as this helps counteract any potential dryness or sensitivity that may occur overnight.

    Can pregnant or breastfeeding individuals use retinol creams?

    No, pregnant or breastfeeding individuals should avoid using retinol creams as retinol gets absorbed into the bloodstream and can be transferred into the milk.
