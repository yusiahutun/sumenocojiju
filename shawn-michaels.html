Biography Timeline

Shawn Michaels, born Michael Shawn Hickenbottom, is a retired professional wrestler who has significantly impacted the wrestling world. He is a four-time world champion and was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame in 2011. Here is a detailed biography timeline for Shawn Michaels:

  • July 22, 1965: Shawn Michaels is born in Chandler, Arizona.
  • 1984: Shawn Michaels begins his wrestling career in Texas, wrestling for the National Wrestling Alliance (NWA).
  • 1987: Shawn Michaels begins wrestling for the American Wrestling Association (AWA).
  • 1988: Shawn Michaels signs with the World Wrestling Federation (WWF) and forms a tag team with Marty Jannetty called The Rockers.
  • 1992: Shawn Michaels becomes the Intercontinental Champion for the first time, defeating The British Bulldog.
  • 1995: Shawn Michaels becomes the first wrestler to win the Royal Rumble from the number one spot.
  • 1996: Shawn Michaels wins his first WWF World Heavyweight Championship, defeating Bret Hart in a 60-minute Iron Man Match at WrestleMania XII.
  • 1997: Shawn Michaels suffers a back injury and is forced to relinquish the WWF World Heavyweight Championship.
  • 1998: Shawn Michaels leads the group D-Generation X feuds with Stone Cold Steve Austin.
  • 1998-2000: Shawn Michaels takes a hiatus from wrestling due to his back injury.
  • 2002: Shawn Michaels returns to wrestling and feuds with Triple H.
  • 2004: Shawn Michaels wins the World Heavyweight Championship for the first time, defeating Triple H in a Last Man Standing match at the Royal Rumble.
  • 2008: Shawn Michaels retires from wrestling after losing to The Undertaker at WrestleMania XXIV.
  • 2010: Shawn Michaels is inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame.
  • 2018: Shawn Michaels returns to compete in a tag team match at Crown Jewel.

In addition to his in-ring career, Shawn Michaels has made several appearances in movies and TV shows. He has also worked as a trainer for the WWE and is involved in various philanthropic endeavours.

Early Life and Childhood

Shawn came into this world on July 22, 1965, in Chandler, Arizona, as the youngest child of Richard and Carol Hickenbottom. He grew up with his older brothers, Randy and Scott, and an older sister, Shari. He was an athlete from a young age but became a professional wrestler at 12. The family moved around frequently since Shawn’s father was an Air Force Pilot. Moreover, Hickenbottom did not like Michael’s name, so his family and friends started calling him Shawn.

After his graduation, he attended Southwest Texas State University in San Marcos, Texas. However, he dropped out of college to pursue a career in wrestling entertainment.

Professional Life

After leaving college, he started training under Mexican professional wrestler Jose Lothario. He began as a professional wrestler working for National Wrestling Alliance (NWA) on October 16, 1984. Furthermore, he also worked for the Texas All-Star Wrestling (TASW) and the American Wrestling Association (AWA).

In 1987, he joined World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE) as a member of The Rockers but was fired just after two weeks. Due to this, he had to return to AWA. A year later, the WWE rehired the group, and it soon became popular among women and children. After The Rockers split, the WWE management put him with Sensational Sherri, who also sang the first version of his new theme, ‘Sexy Boy.’

WWE chairman Vince McMahon allegedly allowed him and his friends Diesel, Ramon, and newcomer Hunter Hearst Helmsley (Triple H), known together as The Kliq. After that, Michaels returned to the WWF at the Royal Rumble match in 1996 and received a WWF Championship match in the main event at WrestleMania XII. Furthermore, Michaels suffered severe back injuries during the game against The Undertaker at the 1998 Royal Rumble. Due to this reason, he was forced to retire for the first time on the night after WrestleMania XVI. After his retirement, he served as the WWF Commissioner from November 1998 to June 2000. After an 18-month hiatus, he returned to WWE television in June 2002.

Apart from his wrestling career, he has hosted an outdoor TV show named ‘Shawn Michaels’ MacMillan River Adventures’. Moreover, he even appeared in two films in 2017: ‘The Resurrection of Gavin Stone’ and ‘Pure Country: Pure Heart.’

Personal Life

Shawn Michaels tied the knot with Theresa Lynn Wood in 1988 but divorced amicably in 1994. After that, he met Rebecca Curci, and they exchanged marriage vows on March 31, 1999. Moreover, Shawn and his wife were blessed with two children, Cameron Cade and a daughter, Cheyenne.
