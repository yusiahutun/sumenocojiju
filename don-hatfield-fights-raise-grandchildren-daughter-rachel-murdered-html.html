Don Hatfield fights to raise his three grandchildren after daughter Rachel is murdered

Widowed grandfather fights to raise his three grandchildren after his daughter 'was stabbed to death by her husband'... but now mom of 'killer' also wants custody

A year ago, noted impressionist artist Don Hatfield was looking forward to a quiet retirement in his Napa, California home with jaunts around the world with his wife Janey.

But his life couldn't have turned out any more differently: just a month after losing Janey to cancer in January, he received a call to say his only daughter had been murdered and his son-in-law arrested.

Now the 64-year-old is raising his daughter's three children, Alex, ten months, Ariel, two, and Eva, four, who were inside their parent's home when their father allegedly stabbed their mother.

Stepping up: Don Hatfield, 64, is bringing up his three grandchildren after his daughter was allegedly murdered by her husband earlier this year. But now he is in a custody battle with their paternal grandmother

It comes after Mr Hatfield and his wife defaulted on mortgage payments and lost their home last year, meaning the financial stability of his successful art career has shattered.

Now yet another hurdle has been thrown in his way: Mr Hatfield is being challenge for the custody of his three grandchildren by their father's mother, Janeth Winkler from Iowa.

In the midst of these legal spats and his mourning for his wife and daughter, Mr Hatfield is raising the three youngsters at a newly-rented four-bedroom home in Napa, the Deseret News reported.

He takes the girls to dance classes - which have been provided for free by a local dance school - and stocks up on art supplies, so that they might take to painting like their grandfather and late mother.

Missed: Rachel Hatfield, 37, was stabbed to death in her California home in February. Her three young children, including Eva (right) and Ariel (left), were in the house at the time of the killing

Doting: Don, who is uncertain what the children witnessed, remembers his daughter as adventurous

'This is what it's all about - now,' he told the News as he put his grandson Alex to bed.

'My wife and I were going to walk into old age together, and then my whole world changed. But with God's help, I'm getting stronger... I think we will be fine.'

'Killer': Todd Winkler claims he acted in self defense when his wife attacked him with scissors

Friends of his late daughter Rachel are also rallying around to help make the children's lives as normal as possible in the abnormal circumstances.

They help feed the children, watch them while their grandfather runs out on errands or pour money into a fund for their care. 'I'm not doing all of this alone,' Hatfield told the News.

Rachel's friend Eva Tagore, who intends to help out for as long as Mr Hatfield needs it, added: 'Rachel's children are in loving hands.'

Speaking to the Deseret News, Mr Hatfield remembered the startling phone call from one of his daughter's neighbours telling him about the stabbing.

Todd Winkler, the man arrested for allegedly killing Rachel, has since protested his innocence, claiming he acted in self defense after the couple had an argument.

Moments after Mr Hatfield found out about her murder, he drove from Napa to her home in Cameron Park, California and collected his three grandchildren. The following day, he appeared at a hearing to petition for temporary guardianship.

At the same time as making final arrangements for Rachel and her funeral, he met with lawyers and began talking to county child protection authorities about his grandchildren's futures.

But that future is uncertain: Janeth Winkler filed for general guardianship of the children, according to her lawyer, Lilka Martinez.

Fighter: Just a month before his daughter was murdered, Mr Hatfield lost his wife to cancer

'In good hands': Mr Hatfield now cares for Ava, four, (left), Ariel, two, and Alex, 10 months (right)

After an investigation into the backgrounds of each of the competing sides, the court will decide who will get custody of the youngsters, Martinez said.

Hatfield is working hard to show authorities his home is the best place for the children, collecting the appropriate paperwork on his income and background.

He is working towards medical insurance for the children, counseling and places in schools. The hearing is in early April, the Desert News reported.

He does not know exactly how much the grandchildren witnessed of his mother's murder, but said that four-year-old Eva recently said: 'Rachel is dead. Mommy is dead.'

Not giving up: Mr Hatfield, pictured with his daughter Rachel, gets help and funds from her friends

Friends said that while Rachel was unhappy in the relationship - as her husband often worked away - there were no signs of physical abuse and the 37-year-old never expressed fears of any.

Winkler's defense team have said it was Rachel who went after their client with a pair of scissors. The 'successful businessman', a U.S. Air Force Academy graduate, acted in self defense, they said.

Attorney David Weiner: 'This man has an exceptional background, and has never been in trouble in his life.'

Apprehensive to talk about the case ahead of a trial, Mr Hatfield simply remembered his business graduate daughter who loved the outdoors as having 'such a spark of life'.

Rachel was 'the spiritual and psychological heart of our family', he added.
