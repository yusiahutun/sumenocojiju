Rachel Weisz reveals why she wouldn't co-star with husband Daniel Craig again

Publish date: 2024-04-04

The couple are three-time co-stars, on Les Grandes Horizontales, Dream House, and Betrayal

    While once co-stars, Rachel Weisz and her husband Daniel Craig are keeping their relationship away from the cameras.

    The couple, who have been married since 2011, first met over a decade before they started dating, as part of the cast of at the National Theatre Studio in 1994. They were co-stars once again on the horror film , during which they quietly started dating, and then once more – seemingly for the last time – on on Broadway, in 2013.

    While no strangers to being both partners in real life and on stage or in front of the camera, now, they would like to keep the two separate.

    MORE: Daniel Craig and Rachel Weisz look cosy at afterparty during very rare outing

    During an appearance on Sunday Today with Willie Geist, which will air Sunday, April 16, Rachel, 52, opened up about their position on remaining just husband and wife, not co-stars.

    "I think we aren't going to [co-star together] at the moment. I think we really love our private life, as a life, as a family. And then we go to work separately," the actress, who next stars in the psychological thriller series Dead Ringers, said.

    "We really enjoyed that experience," she continued, however, she further said: "I think it's nicer to – it also means we can alternate," referring to their busy schedules, both being actors.

    She explained: "So I can stay home with the family while he works. We can swap out. If we're both doing something at the same time, it's probably less ideal."

    Rachel and Daniel, 55, live between London and New York – both of them hail from England – and they share one daughter together, who they welcomed in 2018, as well as children from previous relationships.

    They own a Cobble Hill, Brooklyn townhouse which they purchased for $6.75 million in 2018, as well as a home in Camden, London.

    Rachel has a 16-year-old son, Henry, with former fiancé director Darren Aronofsky. They started dating in 2001 before getting engaged in 2005, though they never married and split in 2010. Meanwhile, Daniel has a 31-year-old daughter, Ella, from his previous marriage to actress Fiona Loudon.
