The Formation

Birmingham City FC was founded in 1875 as Small Heath Alliance. The club changed its name to Birmingham City FC in 1943.

Birmingham City FC is commonly known as the “Blues,” derived from the club’s traditional blue home kit.

Record Attendance

The highest attendance recorded at St. Andrew’s Stadium was on February 11, 1939, when 66,844 spectators watched Birmingham City FC play Everton FC.

Rivalry with Aston Villa

Birmingham City FC shares a heated rivalry with Aston Villa FC. Matches between the two teams, known as the Second City Derby, are fiercely contested by both sets of fans.

Top Scorer

Trevor Francis holds the record for being Birmingham City FC’s top scorer in a single season, scoring 29 goals in the 1977-1978 campaign.
