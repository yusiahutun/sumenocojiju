Adam Collard's net worth after Love Island as Instagram earnings set to soar

Publish date: 2024-03-23

Love Island fans were shocked to find out a familiar face would be returning to the ITV2 reality show.

Adam Collard, 26, originally appeared on Love Island Season 4 in 2018 – the same series Dani Dyer and Jack Fincham took the crown.

He is one of the most infamous Islanders so far – coupling up with four girls during his time in the villa, finding connections with Kendall Rae-Knight, Rosie Williams, Zara McDermott and Darylle Sargeant.

After leaving the villa he rekindled the romance with Zara, who is now dating Sam Thompson, but split in February 2019.

Since leaving the villa Adam has been very busy – and the show has opened many lucrative doors for him.

Here we take a closer look at how much he's earned since leaving Love Island – including how much he can charge per Instagram post.

Adam's exact wealth is unknown, but his net worth is believed to be somewhere between £800,000 to £4.2million, according to the website Explorenetworth.com.

After emerging from the villa in 2018, Adam embarked on a 61 date tour of the UK, where he is believed to have been paid a whopping £305,000.

That works out at an average of £5,000 per appearance, however, the amount Adam actually netted may have been lower due to his agent's cut.

It's standard for management to take around 20% in fees, meaning his final earnings could have been around £240,000.

Since leaving the villa the personal trainer has also founded his own gym called Sculpt Fitness, which offers top-level equipment, classes, body transformation group training and one-to-one personal training.

A standard monthly membership at the gym costs £80, while group training sessions cost a whopping £240.

In addition to making appearances at events and running his business, Adam can earn a lot of money from social media.

Adam can warn up to £3,804 per sponsored Instagram post, according to comparison site JohnSlots, using the Influencer Marketing Calculator.

A spokesperson for JohnSlots said: "These findings highlight the huge influence that Adam Collard has, even before re-entering the Love Island villa, with a singular Instagram post earning him a significant sum of money.

"Deemed the ‘ultimate bombshell’, Adam Collard made Love Island history books back in 2018, when he coupled with four different girls during his 32-day stay in the villa, leaving the public in disbelief.

"As Adam enters the villa tonight, he can expect his current 936,000 Instagram followers to increase, opening even more doors and granting opportunities to expand his influence further across social media."

He also has a YouTube channel and a podcast called The Lift, Laugh, Life podcast.
