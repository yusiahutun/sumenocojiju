Dane Swan opens up on THAT nude video, Dustin Martin and reveals what his tattoos REALLY mean

Publish date: 2024-07-15

AFL bad boy Dane Swan has opened up about his leaked naked video, his mateship with Richmond superstar Dustin Martin, what his tattoos mean to him and his anger at the persecution of his podcast colleague.

The 2011 Brownlow Medallist spoke candidly on Fox Footy's Open Mike show on some of the difficulties he had overcome through his playing career and post-playing life, and how they have shaped him into who he is today.

When asked if he was embarrassed about anything he had done, Swan touched on the nude video which was leaked in 2017.

The clip showed Swan kissing a woman who then filmed his naked body.

Asked about his regrets, Swan said that the sequence of events that led to the video was among them. 

'They say you live and you learn,' he told the show's host Mike Sheahan on Tuesday.

'All your mistakes, if that's what you want to call them, you learn from them and you try and become a better person from them.'

In July, Swan came under fire and his podcast was cancelled after he and co-hosts Scott Cummings and Ralph Horowitz laughed about terms describing non-consensual sex.

While Cummings publicly apologised for what he said, Swan said that despite all the backlash he believes he did nothing wrong. 

'We were laughing at the absurdity of it, saying that's disgusting - who would do that,' Swan said.

'We weren't saying, "go out and do this and have a laugh and report back", so that annoyed me.'

Swan remained strongly loyal to his podcast colleagues, as he did of Tigers star Dustin Martin.

The pair had holidayed together regularly in Las Vegas, and bonded over their lives as much-scrutinised and often misunderstood footy stars.

The famous duo have also been spotted partying in other locations in the United States, with a trip to Austin, Texas, making headlines in 2017. 

Sheahan asked Swan if he and Martin were 'tight'.

'Nah, we hate each other Mike,' Swan joked, at which point Sheahan quickly acknowledged he'd asked a 'silly question'.   

Sheahan asked if Swan was a mentor to Martin, but he insisted that 'Dusty' did not need any advice with how to play footy. 

'He doesn't need me telling him how to play football,' Swan said.

'He's got his head screwed on well and he's got a lot of mentors at the footy club, he's flying now.' 

Swan and Martin share a love for tattoos, and Sheahan asked where the obsession with inking the skin came from.

'When I was growing up tattoos never interested me, but I'm a very impulsive person... I woke up one morning at 22/23 and said "I'm going to get a tattoo",' Swan said.

'I wasn't blessed with beautiful olive skin, so I might as well cover myself in colour,' he joked.

Swan is considered one of Collingwood's greatest midfielders, winning a Brownlow Medal in 2011 and a premiership in 2010 in a 258-game AFL career.

The hard-running star retired in 2016 after badly injuring his foot during a round-one clash with Sydney.
