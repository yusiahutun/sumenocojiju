Inside Arnold Schwarzenegger's huge mansion - roaming animals, tennis court and grotto

Publish date: 2024-03-16

Arnold Schwarzenegger has lived a pretty extravagant life and his humble abodes have been no different.

After meeting his (now ex) wife Maria Shriver, the niece of former president John Kennedy, they lived in a 4,100-square-foot home in the upscale Pacific Palisades neighborhood of Los Angeles.

The sprawling property was privately tucked away on a country lane that meanders off of Sunset Boulevard in Los Angeles is a small community of 16 secluded homes that has attracted world-famous politicians, actors and industry leaders.

The five-bedroom, six-bath home has skylights, three fireplaces, a sauna, spa, grotto-style pool and a lighted tennis court in a setting of ancient oak, sycamore and eucalyptus trees.

After the split, Maria moved into her own house nearby to keep the kids on a regular co-parenting schedule.

The Terminator star then sold the former family home to Maria’s cousins, Maxwell “Max” Kennedy and Vicki Strauss Kennedy.

According to PEOPLE, their former family home has hit the market again in 2021.

The actor then bought a property that was just up the road and equally as impressive Closer Weekly reports.

The impressive property sits on 2.5 acres in one of LA’s most exclusive gated communities and featured a lot of beautiful features.

The abode has seven bedrooms and seven bathrooms, the hillside compound offers a chef’s kitchen, various living rooms, areas for entertainment and a private gym.

The backyard also includes a large pool with a spa, a tennis court, a duck pond and several balconies with jaw-dropping views of the mountains and the ocean.

As well as being massively involved in her children's lives Arnold also has plenty of animals wandering around his exceptional property.

This includes a miniature horse named Whiskey and a donkey named Lulu, that are a huge hit with his fans.

Arnold delighted the internet when he shared a video of himself and two of his pets.

The video encouraged Californians to stay indoors as COVID-19 cases were increasing at the time.

One month later, Arnold announced that he was selling Whiskey and Lulu T-shirts.

The star also owns twos dog named Dutch and Cherry.

According to Danny DeVito, who is a long-term friend of the star, he once told Insider: "The animals just roam around all over the place — they're coming to getcha! Ha ha!

"It's, like, 'What's that?' 'Oh, it's a horse.' 'A HORSE?!' Arnold loves his animals, and he still smokes his stogies."
