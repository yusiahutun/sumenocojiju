The Wilds Star Actress Helena Howard Biography

Helena Howard (born on July 23, 1998) is a 23-years-old American actress who is praised for her acting in shows like “Madeline’s Madeline” and “Social Distance” and since 2020, she has been gaining attention for playing the role of Nora Reid in the Prime famous series “The Wilds.”

Prior to gaining attention after joining the cast of “The Wilds” Howard first time came into the spotlight through her fabulous performance in her life debut film “Madeline’s Madeline,” for which she won Special Jury Prize, MCFCA Award, and got the got nomination IFJA, ICS, IFJA, GWNYFCA, Gotham Independent Film, Independent Spirit and Chlotrudis Awards.

Childhood, Parents, and Education

On July 23, 1998, Kaitlyn Helena Howard (age 23) took birth at the home of her Scandinavian mother and African American father in New York City, U.S. After the birth of Helena, the Howard family got shifted to New Jersey. Like the actress, her father, Will Howard, and mother, Julia Binet, are artists. She also has a sibling, a younger brother named Liam.

Since the age of 3, it was Helena’s dream to become an actor, which is why she started training early at Union County Academy for Performing Arts (from 2012 to 2016). At UCAPA, she got to meet many filmmakers and acting artists.

Career

When she was 18, she met Josephine Decker. The latter acknowledged Howard’s talent and gave her a chance to act in her production named “Madeline’s Madeline” In this show, the role of Helena was Madeline, and the show got so popular that it won her the Gotham Award.

After her debut show, she never looked back and continued to progress. In 2019 she appeared in her second movie called “Twist.” It was an Aly Migliori-directed short movie where Howard played the lead role, a character named Hannah.

Besides appearing in a few more short movies, she also appeared in shows. Her first acting in a T.V. show was in a serial named “Don’t Look Deeper,” a sci-fi drama created by Jeffrey Lieber. She also performed in “Shoplifters of the World” and 2020’s “Social Distance.”
