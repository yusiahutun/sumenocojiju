Stephen Jackson is a former professional basketball player in the United States.  Stephen Jackson spent 14 seasons with the New Jersey Nets, Atlanta Hawks, Indiana Pacers, Golden State Warriors, Milwaukee Bucks, Charlotte Bobcats, San Antonio Spurs, and Los Angeles Clippers in the National Basketball Association (NBA). Moreover, In 2003, Jackson won an NBA championship with the San Antonio Spurs.

Early Life

High School Career

In his junior year, Jackson led Lincoln High School to a state championship. While at Lincoln, he was academically ineligible; however, he transferred to Oak Hill Academy (Virginia), where he was recognized as an All-American in 1996. He was the top scorer on his squad, which also included Kobe Bryant, Jermaine O’Neal, and Tim Thomas, in the 1996 McDonald’s All-American Boys Game.

Jackson committed to enroll at the University of Arizona, but his low SAT and ACT results were used to determine his academic eligibility. Instead, he spent one semester at Butler County Community College in El Dorado, Kansas, but did not play basketball there.
